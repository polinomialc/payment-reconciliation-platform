# Receipts and Payment Batches

## Purpose

Explain the two sides of the reconciliation process.

## Receipt

A receipt is external payment evidence from the payment or banking side.

It can represent:

- successful customer payment
- rejected card transaction
- refund
- chargeback
- cancellation-fee movement
- adjustment

## Payment Batch

A payment batch is the internal grouped payment object that needs allocation or review.

When receipt evidence matches the payment batch by reference, amount, sign, and date rules, the batch can move toward allocation.

When evidence is incomplete, the batch remains open and enters aging review.

## Operating Principle

The receipt explains what happened in the payment channel.

The payment batch explains what the finance process expects to allocate.

Reconciliation connects those two views.
